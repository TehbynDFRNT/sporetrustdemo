Motion

RESOLVED: Proceed with Sporetrust/SporeTrace as a 60-day validation sprint, not a full-scale business launch, with go/no-go gates on CAC, paid bookings, subscription conversion, referral compliance, and delivery operations.

The original question, "Should I do this business?", is too broad. The correct decision is whether to validate a staged operating model before committing scale capital, brand risk, contractors, or legal exposure.

Evidence Read

Business Case

The demo models diagnostic acquisition, annual onsite monitoring, downstream referral revenue, and future in-house testing. Assumptions include $995 diagnostic price, $150 booked CAC, 50% subscription conversion, and 25% annual churn [UNVERIFIED].

Live App

The app sells one-off diagnostics at $695 and $945 in Brisbane/SEQ, targets tenants, homeowners, and property managers, and routes bookings through Cal.com. The annual monitoring product that drives LTV is not present.

Material Mismatch

The site says Sporetrust does not clip the cleanup ticket, while the business case assumes remediation and builder referral revenue. That contradiction blocks a full launch until positioning, disclosure, and compliance are settled.

CEO Ruling

RATIFIED: majority stands. The binding outcome is to proceed with a 60-day validation sprint only. No national rollout, no scaled ad spend, no referral-fee model, and no annual monitoring forecast should be treated as real until the proof gates below pass.

Accepted risk: a slower staged decision may let a competitor move first. Rejected risk: scaling an unvalidated services business with unresolved independence, referral, subscription, and operational assumptions.

Binding Next Action

  1. Fix the proposition in 7 days: decide whether the company is independent diagnostics only, diagnostics plus disclosed referral marketplace, or diagnostics plus annual monitoring. The website, FAQ, report sample, and business model must match one position.
  2. Add the missing recurring offer in 14 days: test an annual onsite monitoring plan at $695 or an alternative price, with clear inclusions, renewal terms, cancellation terms, and delivery cost assumptions.
  3. Run paid-demand validation for 60 days: require at least 20 paid diagnostic bookings from cold channels, booked CAC at or below $250, cancellation/refund rate below 15%, and evidence that $150 CAC is reachable with channel optimisation.
  4. Run subscription validation inside the same sprint: offer monitoring to every diagnostic customer and require at least 30% conversion or paid deposit intent before treating the recurring LTV case as investable.
  5. Clear legal and operating gates before referral revenue: document referral disclosures, customer consent, contractor vetting, report disclaimers, insurance/liability position, privacy handling, and conflict controls before taking any cleanup or builder referral fee.
  6. Measure delivery economics: prove COGS at or below $150 per diagnostic, 48-hour standard report delivery, 5-7 day lab addendum delivery where sampled, and 4 quality jobs per technician per day before hiring against the 30-tech model.

Re-vote Condition

The board should re-vote after 60 days with actual booked CAC, conversion, COGS, cycle time, refund rate, complaint rate, partner/referral compliance status, and first subscription sales. A full launch motion requires at least 4 of 6 proof gates to pass and no GC blocker.
