Motion

RESOLVED: The Board has confidence in the medium-term Sporetrust/SporeTrace business propositions as directionally investable, provided the financial case is re-underwritten with lower attachment rates, a 10-tech/6-jobs-per-day operating case, and gated proof before scaling beyond validated demand.

This is not a vote to accept the exact 30-staff, high-attachment, or 8-jobs-per-day upside case. It is a vote that the propositions remain commercially valid after normalising scale and attachment assumptions.

CEO Reframe

Question Being Answered

Are the medium-term propositions still credible after haircutting attachment rates and using a lower staffing case?

Question Not Being Answered

Does the Board believe the exact Year-5 national upside model, 30 staff at 8 jobs/day, or every downstream attach assumption?

CEO Position

Yes on proposition validity. No on using the top-end scenario as a planning base. The operating model is easy to over-scale because each technician produces high annual volume.

CEO Ruling

RATIFIED: majority stands. The Board has confidence in the medium-term propositions, with the binding constraint that the financial model must be revised downward before it is used for capital allocation, hiring, partnership commitments, or paid media scale.

Accepted risk: haircutting attachment rates and staffing scale may understate upside. Rejected risk: treating an easy-to-over-scale technician model as if market penetration, subscription conversion, and referral economics are already proven.

Binding Outcome

  • Confidence granted: independent diagnostics, annual monitoring, evidence-grade reports, and a controlled downstream pathway are valid medium-term propositions.
  • Base model revised: use 10 technicians, 6 jobs per technician per day, 13,200 diagnostics per year, 30% annual monitoring conversion, and a 50% haircut to downstream attachment economics until data proves otherwise [UNVERIFIED].
  • Upside demoted: 30 technicians at 8 jobs per day remains a sensitivity case only. It is not the planning case and should not drive hiring, spend, or valuation language.
  • Referral revenue capped: do not rely on remediation or builder referral fees until the independence claim, disclosures, consent, and legal controls are reconciled.
  • Scaling gate: expand beyond 10 technicians only after utilisation, booked CAC, churn, subscription conversion, COGS, report QA, and complaint rates pass two consecutive operating periods.

Next Action

  1. Revise the financials HTML into a conservative board case with three tabs or sections: 5-tech pilot, 10-tech medium case, and 30-tech upside sensitivity.
  2. Replace current downstream attachment assumptions with a haircut table: low, medium, and proven-rate cases by revenue stream.
  3. Separate annual monitoring from other attachment rates; it is the strategic recurring layer and needs its own conversion, churn, gross margin, and renewal proof.
  4. Add a hiring rule: no technician added unless trailing utilisation and lead volume support at least 4 booked jobs per technician per day without degrading 48-hour report delivery.
  5. Prepare a re-vote pack when the revised model shows contribution after realistic fixed opex, not only contribution before fixed opex.
